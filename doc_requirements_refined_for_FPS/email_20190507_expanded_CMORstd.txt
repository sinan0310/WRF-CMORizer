Hi Martina,

Finally we have a document for you detailing our proposed file/path naming protocol for convection permitting models (see attached and thank you Ivonne!). I fear it is not very compact but there was a general feeling that the file name should have all the necessary information about the model chain for a user. In the case of our simulations this means: Global Outer domain/Model or Reanalysis -> Outer domain/Intermediate model -> Inner domain/Convection permitting model. It is the information on this “Intermediate” step that is new compared to regular CORDEX simulations. It would be great to get your thoughts on the proposed additions. Also there is at least one potential issue I can already foresee. Like CORDEX we are flexible when it comes to these outer domains. The only requirement is that everybody is on the same mandatory inner domain. As a result a few teams skip the intermediate model and go right to the inner domain. They would either have to remove the intermediate step information from their filename/path or replace these elements with “none”.  My question is: Will this work with the proposed filename/path structure and the requirements of ESGF?

We are not in any rush to get theses simulations up on ESGF but teams are post-processing and we want have output in ESGF compliant form on our storage facility at Juelich (maintained by Klaus Georgen). So it will be good to already have a workable structure already. Also, if this structure is problematic we already have an alternative, more compact solution available where all the nesting information is flagged under the <nesting information> element.  Thank you for taking the time to review this I appreciate it. And thank you to everyone in cc here for your valuable input on this.

Cheers,
Stefan
